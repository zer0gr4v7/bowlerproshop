An online bowling pro shop gives you wider selection and price comparison than most local shops. But not all online retailers are the same. Some offer drilling services, some ship undrilled only, and return policies vary widely for custom-drilled equipment.

## What an online bowling pro shop actually is

Online bowling pro shops sell bowling balls, shoes, bags, and accessories through ecommerce. Some are full-service (offering drilling by mail with measurements you provide), while others sell undrilled equipment that you take to a local pro shop for fitting.

## Top online bowling retailers to compare

When shopping for bowling gear online, compare these established retailers:

- BowlersMart: large inventory, frequent sales, drilling available
- BowlingBall.com: wide ball selection, comparison tools, drilling services
- Bowling.com: broad catalog across all gear categories
- BowlerX: competitive pricing, customer service reputation
- Amazon: convenience, fast shipping, but verify seller reputation for bowling-specific gear

## What to check before buying online

- Return policy for drilled vs undrilled equipment
- Drilling options and measurement requirements
- Shipping cost for heavy items (bowling balls weigh 10-16 lbs)
- Seller reputation and authorized dealer status
- Warranty coverage from the manufacturer

## When to buy online vs at a local pro shop

Buy online when: you know your specs, want price comparison, need accessories/supplies, or your local shop lacks selection.

Buy at a local pro shop when: you need fitting, drilling, layout consultation, surface adjustments, or hands-on advice about your game.

Many experienced bowlers do both: research and compare online, then buy drilled balls through their pro shop for proper fitting and ongoing maintenance.

## Drilling considerations for online ball purchases

If you buy a ball online undrilled, you still need a local pro shop to drill it. Budget $40-$80 for drilling plus inserts. Provide your pro shop with the ball's pin position and preferred layout if you have a specific motion in mind.

## Price comparison tips

- Check multiple retailers for the same model
- Factor in drilling cost if buying undrilled
- Watch for seasonal sales (especially during bowling season start in August-September)
- Compare shipping costs — balls are heavy
- Look for bundle deals (ball + bag, or shoes + accessories)

## FAQ

### Is it safe to buy bowling balls online?

Yes, from established retailers. Verify the seller is an authorized dealer for warranty coverage. For drilled balls purchased online, provide accurate measurements to avoid fit problems.

### Do online bowling pro shops offer returns on drilled balls?

Policies vary significantly. Most retailers do not accept returns on custom-drilled equipment. Some offer exchange programs or store credit. Always check the drilling return policy before ordering.

### How long does shipping take for bowling equipment?

Standard shipping for bowling balls is typically 5-10 business days due to weight. Expedited options are available from most retailers. Accessories and supplies usually ship faster and cheaper.
